Hi Richard,

Maciek fwded your email to me and here's at least some initial
feedback
- assuming you haven't already figured out some solutions.  First,
the
reason that the script was rendering such "thick" border lines was
because of the renWin.SetSize(--).  I guess you were using the
actual
size of the lattice - or something close to that?  If you simply
increase the rendering window resolution, e.g. (512,512) then the
default mapper on the border lines (1 pixel) will be more
meaningful.
I
actually preferred the following, but whatever:
bactor.GetProperty().SetLineWidth(2.0)

The other problem of mis-registration between cell borders and
pixels
most likely has to do with the quirky thing in cc3D to add an extra
space to the lattice (I don't claim to thoroughly understand the
reasons
behind it).  However, to at least partially solve this problem in
your
script, I found that inserting the following helped (at least with
my
simple test case):
bactor.SetPosition(0.0,-1.0,0.0)

I plan to eventually  post a page related to this on my wiki brain
dump
and will send a link when I do.  I've also attached your script
with
my
tweaks (search for 'rwh').

-Randy

               points = vtk.vtkPoints()
               lines = vtk.vtkCellArray()

        (points)
               linesIntAddr=self.extractAddressIntFromVtkObject
        (lines)

               self.parentWidget.fieldExtractor.fillBorderData2D
        (pointsIntAddr
        , linesIntAddr, self.currentDrawingParameters.plane,
        self.currentDrawingParameters.planePos)

               borders = vtk.vtkPolyData()

               borders.SetPoints(points)
               borders.SetLines(lines)

               self.borderMapper.SetInput(borders)
               _actors[0].SetMapper(self.borderMapper)
